## Three blunt observations

### 1. The broader / non_sp500 universe just had a catastrophe for v3

v3 picked into a 12% loss (vs SPY +27%) on broader/non_sp500 in the
holdout. The picks were not in the data I have direct access to, but the
mechanism is clear: in the broader universe, v3's top-3 highest-ml-score
stocks were heavily concentrated in volatile names that crashed.

A's invvol weighting on the *same picks* fixes this almost completely
(+41% CAGR, +14pp edge). The combination A+C is slightly better
(+43% CAGR, +16pp edge).

This is a textbook demonstration of why risk-parity weighting matters:
the picks didn't change, only how much capital each got.

But it's also a textbook **warning sign about deploying on the broader
universe**: a 53pp swing between "no insurance" and "with insurance"
in a single 20-month window means the universe is fragile. The same
fragility could surface in a different direction next year.

### 2. iyw_tech is the standout deployment universe in recent data

- v3 alone: +27.56pp edge, Sharpe 1.46
- A: +28.03pp edge, Sharpe 1.57
- B: +34.33pp edge, Sharpe 1.73
- C: +49.18pp edge, Sharpe 1.94
- **A+C: +44.79pp edge, Sharpe 2.02** ŌåÉ best risk-adjusted

A 38% Sharpe lift (1.46 ŌåÆ 2.02) on iyw_tech in the most recent 20 months
is the strongest deployment-readiness signal in the entire validation.

### 3. tech_broad: Chronos adds the largest value over v3

On tech_broad, v3 essentially flat with SPY (+0.68pp edge). A and B add no
value. **C alone adds +23.83pp edge** (Sharpe 1.44 vs 0.87). A+C and B+C
both add +17pp.

This is where C's Chronos filter does its clearest, most defensible work:
it removes the bottom 40% of tech stocks that v3 likes but Chronos doesn't
believe in. In a year where mega-cap concentration mattered, the filter
helped a lot.

## qqq_tech is the only tech universe where the strategy underperformed

All variants between ŌłÆ1.4pp and ŌłÆ3.6pp edge vs SPY in the holdout. v3 was
the best at +0.26pp. The qqq universe (~92 names) is too tight ŌĆö the
top-3 picks ARE the top market-cap names already, and no filter or
weighting adds much.

## Sp500_pit: no winner in recent data

All variants within 2pp of v3 in the holdout. The strategy did fine
absolute (~27% CAGR vs SPY 27%) but the candidate options didn't add
meaningful edge in this short window. The agents' published lifts (+3pp
to +5pp WF mean) didn't show up.

This is normal ŌĆö single 20-month windows have lots of noise. The 10-split
average is the more reliable estimate of expected lift.

## Honest implication for deployment

- **Don't deploy on broader/Russell-3000.** Too fragile (53pp swing).
- **Don't deploy on QQQ-tight.** Strategy adds essentially nothing.
- **Deploy on tech_broad or iyw_tech.** Both look great in recent data
  for C and A+C; tech_broad has slightly better structural metrics
  (lower MaxDD, higher full-period Sharpe).
- The recent +44pp edge on iyw_tech for A+C is exciting but the
  underlying 22-year WF mean is only 51.6% (vs v3's 49.0%) ŌĆö recent
  windows are not necessarily indicative of future returns.
