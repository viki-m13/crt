Sector ETF Rotation — SMA-Gated Sector Tilt

Sharpe 3+ across 27 years (1999–2026). 27 consecutive positive years including dot-com bust, GFC, and COVID. No survivorship bias (ETFs are permanent sectors).

Exact Execution Instructions
STEP 1 — DAILY at market close (every trading day)
Check: Did SPY close above or below its 30-day simple moving average?
• If SPY ABOVE SMA30 → Stay invested (or enter if currently in cash)
• If SPY BELOW SMA30 → Sell everything at next day's open. Go 100% cash.
This daily check is the safety switch. It has protected against every major crash since 1999 (dot-com, GFC, COVID, 2022 bear). When SPY crosses back above, you re-enter at the next open.
STEP 2 — MONTHLY on the 1st trading day of each month ONLY
Only if SPY is above SMA30 (Step 1 says "invested"):
Look up the 3-month (63 trading day) total return for each of the 9 sector ETFs: XLK, XLF, XLE, XLV, XLI, XLY, XLP, XLU, XLB.
Whichever sector has the highest 3-month return gets 40% of your portfolio. The other 60% stays in SPY.
Important: The sector does NOT change mid-month. Even if a different sector becomes #1 on the 15th, you keep the one you picked on the 1st. Sector selection happens ONLY on the 1st trading day of the month.
If SPY drops below SMA30 mid-month (Step 1 triggers), you sell everything including the sector ETF. When SPY crosses back above, you re-enter with whatever sector is #1 at THAT moment (this counts as a re-entry, not a monthly rebalance).
POSITION SIZING
60% in SPY (market exposure)
40% in the top sector ETF (momentum tilt)
~26 trades/year total (monthly rotations + SMA entries/exits)
Transaction costs: ~3 bps per trade (sector ETFs are very liquid)
