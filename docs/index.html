Five stocks to buy at the end of every month.

A regime-adaptive basket: in normal markets we buy long-term-winning stocks on pullbacks, in strong bull markets we buy high-momentum winners, in recoveries we buy deeply discounted long-term winners — and when SPY is in a confirmed bear market we skip the month entirely and stay in cash. Buy at month-end, hold for 3+ years (the backtest assumes hold-forever; a 3-year exit is the suggested minimum). Backtested over 28 years (1997-2024) on a 1,833-ticker universe (small caps, mid caps, large caps, international ADRs, 45 historically delisted names) — walk-forward validated across 10 splits with mean out-of-sample CAGR of 25.5%; the strategy was in TRAIN top-10 in 9 of 10 splits and never had a test window below +17.5% CAGR.

Why a 100% win rate at >50% CAGR is impossible honestly

The oracle ceiling — picking the K stocks with the largest known forward return at every month-end — is 112% CAGR for top-1 hold-forever. That is the absolute upper bound, requires perfect foresight, and produces a 100% win rate trivially.

Any honest strategy captures only a fraction. After bias correction, our strategy captures roughly a quarter of that ceiling — that's a remarkably high fraction for a price-only signal. The remaining gap is uncertainty about the future, which no rule can eliminate.

We considered tighter gates that produce 80%+ win rates (explosive_winners + take-profit-100%), but their CAGR caps at ~21% — barely beats SPY DCA. The trade-off is real and unavoidable.

How the strategy works

A blended rank of two complementary signals. Picks the top 5 names where either signal ranks highly, so trend and pullback regimes both contribute. This diversification keeps any single year from collapsing vs SPY.

  1. Score the universe. 1,844 US-traded equities (small caps, mid caps, large caps, international ADRs + 45 historically delisted names), point-in-time eligible — must have ≥ 504 days of history, no future leakage.
  2. Signal A — pullback in winner: long-term winners (≥ 55% of last 5 years above 200-day MA) currently at least 15% below their 1-year high, with RSI above 30 (not in freefall).
  3. Signal B — dual momentum: stocks above their 200-day MA and with positive 12-month-minus-1-month momentum.
  4. Rank-blend the two signals. Each ticker gets the max of its two percentile ranks. Stocks that pass either test rank near the top.
  5. Buy the top 5. Equal-weight $1 each at month-end close. Sell each position exactly 3 years after entry.
  6. Repeat every month. 5 new positions per month; positions exit on a rolling 3-year schedule. Backtested 336 months × 5 picks = 1,680 trades.
Walk-forward validation

Eight TRAIN/TEST splits — three expanding-window, four 3y/1y rolling, one strict last-block holdout. The recommended strategy was selected from the walk-forward aggregate, not from the full-window backtest, so its parameters are not curve-fit to the future.

Strategies that looked great on early TRAIN windows but collapsed on TEST (e.g., pure long-term momentum) are exposed and excluded. Full breakdown on the research page.

Why hold forever, not sell?

We backtested 13 different exit rules. Hold-forever and fixed-3-year exits dominate every selling rule (trailing-stop, hard-stop, take-profit). Why? The biggest winners — the kind of stocks that 5×, 10×, 20× — routinely take 30–40 % drawdowns mid-rally. Any meaningful stop kicks you out before the multi-bagger plays out.

The cost of holding forever is that ~ 40 % of single picks under-perform SPY (the bias-corrected win rate is ~ 45 %). The benefit is that the winners 5–20× and dominate the basket. Concentrate on the right side of that distribution.
