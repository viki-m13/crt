A fresh stock basket every month — chosen by ML.

A walk-forward Gradient Boosted Trees ranking model picks the top stocks from a 1,833-ticker universe each month, and a crash-aware regime gate shifts the entire portfolio to cash when SPY breaks down. The model is fit on 67 cross-sectionally rank-transformed price-only features (momentum, trend health, recovery rate, idiosyncratic momentum, RSI, breakout strength, etc.) and trained at three forward horizons (1m / 3m / 6m) — predictions are ensembled. Top-15 picks in normal markets, top-7 in confirmed bull or recovery, cash in confirmed crash. Capital fully rebalances each month-end so returns compound. Backtested 2003-2024 on the full 1,833-ticker universe — walk-forward validated across 10 splits with mean out-of-sample CAGR of 51.8%, beating SPY DCA by an average of +39pp. Bias-corrected at α=4%/yr delisting: 74.4% CAGR median.

Why a 100% win rate at >50% CAGR is impossible honestly

The oracle ceiling — picking the K stocks with the largest known forward return at every month-end — is 112% CAGR for top-1 hold-forever. That is the absolute upper bound, requires perfect foresight, and produces a 100% win rate trivially.

Any honest strategy captures only a fraction. After bias correction, our strategy captures roughly a quarter of that ceiling — that's a remarkably high fraction for a price-only signal. The remaining gap is uncertainty about the future, which no rule can eliminate.

We considered tighter gates that produce 80%+ win rates (explosive_winners + take-profit-100%), but their CAGR caps at ~21% — barely beats SPY DCA. The trade-off is real and unavoidable.

How the strategy works

A walk-forward Gradient Boosted Trees ranking model picks the top stocks each month, with a crash-aware regime gate that shifts to cash when the market breaks down.

  1. Score the universe. 1,833 US-traded equities (small caps, mid caps, large caps, international ADRs), point-in-time eligible — must have ≥ 252 days of history, no future leakage. Ticker-reuse and adjustment-error months are detected and masked.
  2. Cross-sectionally rank features. 67 price-only features (momentum, trend health, recovery rate, idiosyncratic momentum, RSI, volatility, breakout strength, drawdown profile, etc.) are converted to per-month percentile ranks in [-1, +1] so the model only learns relative orderings — regime-free.
  3. Train three Gradient Boosted Trees models. Each predicts the cross-sectional rank of forward returns at 1m / 3m / 6m horizons. Refit every January on all data older than (test month − 7 months) so labels never leak across the cutoff. Predictions are ensembled.
  4. Classify SPY regime. Crash if SPY 21d ≤ -8% or 6m ≤ -5% AND 21d ≤ -3%. Recovery if SPY just reclaimed its 200dma after a 40d+ below-streak. Bull if SPY 12m mom ≥ 10% AND above 200dma. Else Normal.
  5. Pick top-K based on regime. Crash → 100% cash. Recovery → top-7. Bull → top-7. Normal → top-15. Equal-weight within picks.
  6. Monthly rebalance. Each month-end, sell everything and redeploy into the new top-K. Returns from prior winners compound into the next month's high-conviction names. 10 bp/month turnover cost applied.
Walk-forward validation

10 TRAIN/TEST splits covering 2003-2024 — three expanding-window (A1/A2/A3), six 3y rolling (R1-R6) including the GFC, COVID, 2022 bear and AI rally, plus one strict last-block holdout (STRICT). The model is fit only on TRAIN data with a 7-month embargo gap.

Aggregate (TEST): 10/10 positive splits, mean OOS CAGR 51.8%, mean edge +39.4pp over SPY DCA, 9/10 beat SPY DCA. Min OOS CAGR 11.2% (R6 AI rally — equal-weighted small/mid-cap basket lags mega-cap-dominated SPY); max OOS CAGR 162.9% (R1 GFC bottom recovery). Full breakdown on the research page.

Why monthly rebalance?

720 variants tested in the v2 sweep (5 K-normal × 3 K-recovery × 4 K-bull × 2 conviction × 2 cash-gate × 5 regime modes). Monthly rebalance with the winner config (K-normal=15, equal-weight, tight crash gate) dominates because the ML score's predictive power is concentrated at the 1-3 month horizon — holding picks longer than that decays the signal. The cost is monthly turnover (10bp/month modeled) and tax inefficiency in taxable accounts.

Survivorship-bias overlay

The universe includes only tickers alive today. To honestly account for the survivors-only bias, we run a Monte Carlo overlay where each pick has an independent per-month probability of being synthetically wiped to -100% (calibrated to the historical small-cap delisting hazard rate α). At α=4%/yr (the historical default), median CAGR is 74.4% (p10 71%, p90 77%). Even at α=12%/yr (3× historical), median CAGR is 59.3%.
